# n=9 high-risk frontier packet

Status: `REVIEW_PENDING_DIAGNOSTIC` / proof-mining packet. No proof of
`n=9`, no general proof of Erdos Problem #97, and no counterexample are
claimed.

This packet isolates the mutual-edge-triangle part of the existing `n=9`
pre-vertex-circle frontier. It is meant to answer a narrow next-step question:
among the 184 selected-witness assignments, which ones most resemble the hard
`n=8` residue where metric equalities are plausible but convexity or
vertex-circle constraints do the real work?

## Selection rule

Start from the 184 complete `n=9` assignments generated by
`src/erdos97/n9_vertex_circle_exhaustive.py` before vertex-circle pruning.
Select an assignment when:

```text
the mutual-edge graph contains at least one triangle, and
the simple mutual-edge obstruction-A trigger is absent.
```

Here a mutual edge means `i in S_j` and `j in S_i`. The simple obstruction-A
trigger is the n=8-style local metric trap:

```text
i <-> j,
S_i and S_j share witnesses a,b,
and one shared witness row selects the other shared witness plus endpoint i or j.
```

All 184 `n=9` frontier assignments have zero such triggers, so this simple
obstruction is too weak on the current frontier. The triangle condition
therefore picks out the high-risk mutual-edge motifs rather than a killed
subslice.

## Replay checks

For every selected fixed-row assignment, the packet records:

- the mutual edges and mutual-edge triangles;
- zero simple obstruction-A triggers;
- the first size-4 radius blocker;
- a fixed-row radius-blocker replay;
- the vertex-circle kill reason, either `vertex_circle_self_edge` or
  `vertex_circle_strict_cycle`.

Because each row is fixed, every radius-blocker replay has exactly one raw row
selection. The point of the replay is not combinatorial branching; it is a
cross-check that the blocker packet interface, incidence filters, and
vertex-circle quotient replay agree on the same high-risk slice.

## Research use

This packet says the natural n=8-inspired mutual-edge obstruction does not
touch the mutual-edge-triangle slice of the `n=9` frontier. The useful next
target is therefore not more simple-A searching. It is to explain the five
families above by reusable local-core or row-Ptolemy arguments, especially the
two non-ear `F16` representatives.

The packet remains a finite diagnostic. It does not promote the review-pending
`n=9` checker to a public theorem-style claim.
