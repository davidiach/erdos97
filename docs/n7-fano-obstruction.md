# n=7 Fano-incidence obstruction

In the `n=7` equality case, the selected-witness complements form pointed Fano
lines. For every center chord `{i,j}`, the two common witnesses
`S_i cap S_j` form another chord, giving a permutation of the 21 unordered
chords. In any geometric realization, the center chord must be perpendicular
to its common-witness chord, since the two centers lie on that chord's
perpendicular bisector.

Every permutation of 21 objects has an odd cycle. Alternating perpendicularity
around an odd cycle is impossible for nonzero Euclidean chords, so the
`n=7` equality case cannot be geometrically realized.

See `docs/n7-fano-enumeration.md` for the full enumerator note, canonical
representatives, and reproducible JSON artifact.
